## Three-statement model playbook

### Driver architecture (bottom-up)

```
                         Drivers
        ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
        ▼                                        ▼
   REVENUE DRIVERS                       EXPENSE DRIVERS
   - New logos × ACV (SaaS)              - Headcount × cost (S&M / R&D / G&A)
   - Traffic × conv × AOV (e-com)        - Rev × COGS % (hosting + support + payment)
   - GMV × take-rate (marketplace)       - Headcount × space × $/sqft (rent)
   - Cohort NRR (expansion + churn)      - LLM inference $/call × volume (AI-native)
        │                                        │
        ▼                                        ▼
        REVENUE                                  COSTS
        │                                        │
        └─────────────┐         ┌────────────────┘
                      ▼         ▼
                     P&L (revenue − COGS − OpEx = EBITDA − D&A − I = Net Income)
                      │
                      ▼
                     BS (assets = liabilities + equity; cash from CF; retained earnings += NI)
                      │
                      ▼
                     CF (Operating + Investing + Financing = Δ Cash)
```

### Tie-out rules

- Net Income from P&L → Retained Earnings on BS
- Δ Cash from CF → Cash on BS (opening + Δ = closing)
- Δ AR / AP / Prepaid / Accrued from BS → Operating CF working-capital lines
- Capex from BS PP&E roll → Investing CF
- Debt raise / repayment / equity issuance from BS → Financing CF

## Driver-based revenue playbook

### SaaS (new + expansion + churn)

```
Month-N Revenue = (Month-N-1 Revenue × (1 + Expansion% − Churn%))
                 + (New Customers × Initial ACV / 12)
```

Cohort triangle: rows = cohort month; columns = months since acquisition; cell = revenue from that cohort.

Standard 2026 cohort survival curve: SMB ~70% at 12mo / 50% at 24mo; mid-market ~85% at 12mo / 75% at 24mo; enterprise ~95% at 12mo / 90% at 24mo.

### E-commerce (traffic × conversion × AOV × repeat)

```
Period Revenue = New Buyers × AOV + Repeat Buyers × Repeat Frequency × AOV
                 (where Repeat Buyers = Prior Cohort × Repeat Rate)
```

### Marketplace (GMV × take-rate)

```
Period Revenue = GMV × Take Rate
                 (where GMV = Supplier-side volume × Average Transaction Size)
```

### Common pitfalls (red flags in models)

- Aggregating all cohorts into "average customer" — hides SMB churn behind enterprise stickiness
- Using churn from 6 months ago as forward churn — recent cohort is what matters
- Forecasting expansion without naming the motion driving it
- Smoothing seasonality in B2B SaaS (year-end deal pull-forwards are real)

## Scenario and Monte Carlo playbook

### Three-scenario discipline

| Scenario | What's flexed | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Management expected case | Investor pitch baseline; board commit |
| Bull | +20% growth / +15% conv / +10% NRR | Upside framing; opportunity cost analysis |
| Bear | −30% growth / −20% conv / −10% NRR | Runway planning; raise sizing |

### Sensitivity tornado (top 5 drivers)

For startup runway:
1. CAC (often ±20% drives ±15% runway months)
2. Churn (±20% drives ±10% runway)
3. ACV (±20% drives ±8% runway)
4. Burn (±20% drives ±20% runway — linear)
5. Hiring pace (±20% drives ±15% runway)

Tornado chart: drivers ranked by sensitivity magnitude; widest bar = highest priority to manage.

### Monte Carlo (when to use)

Use Monte Carlo when:
- Decision is high-stakes (raise sizing, runway-to-zero risk)
- Top drivers have wide distributions (e.g., new product launch, untested geography)
- Single-point scenarios feel like guessing

Don't use Monte Carlo when:
- Mature, predictable business — 3-scenario tornado is enough
- User is decision-making in 24 hours — sensitivity is faster
- Audience is board / investors — they want story, not probability density

Python recipe (`uvx scipy numpy`):
```python
import numpy as np
from scipy import stats
n = 10000
cac = stats.lognorm(s=0.3, scale=1000).rvs(n)  # CAC distribution
churn = stats.beta(2, 50).rvs(n)               # monthly churn
arpu = stats.norm(100, 15).rvs(n)              # ARPU
runway_months = current_cash / (n_customers * (cac/24) - n_customers * arpu * (1-churn))
print(f"P(runway < 18mo) = {(runway_months < 18).mean():.1%}")
```

## Capital allocation playbook

### Damodaran framework (apply hierarchically)

1. **Reinvest** at marginal ROIC > WACC + risk premium
   - In startups: invest where LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1 AND CAC payback < 18 months
   - Sanity-check: Damodaran 2026 industry cost-of-capital table
2. **Reserve** runway buffer to 24 months
   - 2026 investor expectation: 24-30 months post-funding
3. **Acquire (M&A)** only with strategic fit + accretive economics
   - Premium typically 20-50% over standalone EV; accretion requires synergy realization
4. **Return capital** (dividend / buyback) only after all profitable reinvestment exhausted
   - Rare for startups; relevant for late-stage / public preparation
5. **Leverage opportunistically**
   - Venture debt 20-35% of last equity round when capital-efficient (NRR ≥ 100%, GM > 70%)

### ROIC opportunity scorecard

```
| Opportunity | Capital req | Expected ROIC | WACC + risk prem | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sales hire (US) | $200K (1yr) | 60% (LTV:CAC 5:1) | 25% | Invest |
| Marketing channel expansion | $500K (6mo) | 40% (CAC payback 12mo) | 25% | Invest |
| New product line | $1M (12mo) | 15% (unproven ICP) | 35% (high risk) | Reject |
| Geographic expansion | $800K (12mo) | 25% (transferable motion) | 30% | Hold |
| M&A acquisition | $5M (instant) | 30% (synergy modeled) | 25% | Investigate |

## Fundraising strategy playbook

### Decision tree by stage + revenue posture

```
Pre-seed (no revenue)
  → Post-money SAFE (YC standard) with cap + discount
  → Cap typically $5M-$15M; discount 10-25%

Seed (early revenue, $0-$1M ARR)
  → Post-money SAFE OR priced Seed via NVCA
  → Cap typically $10M-$30M; priced typically $5M-$15M raise

Series A (PMF, $1M-$5M ARR)
  → Priced via NVCA (Oct 2025 update)
  → Lead investor typical $5M-$15M of $10M-$25M round
  → Pre-money typically $20M-$80M

Series B (scaling, $5M-$20M ARR)
  → Priced via NVCA
  → Round size $20M-$50M; pre-money $100M-$400M

Series C+ (growth, $20M+ ARR)
  → Priced via NVCA + possible secondaries
  → Round size $50M-$200M+; pre-money $300M+

Revenue-positive, capital-efficient (any stage)
  → Add venture debt 20-35% of last equity round
  → OR RBF (Capchase / Pipe / Wayflyer / Founderpath) for 30-50% of ARR

Late-stage growth ($100M+ ARR)
  → Add secondaries for founder + early-employee liquidity
  → Forge Global / EquityZen / Carta CrossFlow
```

### 2026 capital-stack landscape

Per Axis Group Ventures, 2026 trend = blended capital stack. Founders no longer choose "equity vs debt" — they engineer multi-instrument stacks:
- Equity (lead VC priced round) + venture debt (20-35% of equity) + RBF (for working capital) + secondaries (for liquidity) + grants / R&D credit (non-dilutive cash flow)

### Cap-table dilution math

Priced round:
- Pre-money = $X; Investment = $Y; Post-money = X + Y
- New investor ownership = Y / (X + Y)
- Existing dilution = X / (X + Y); proportional
- Option pool top-up usually pre-money (refresh dilutes existing more)

SAFE → priced round conversion (YC post-money):
- Cap = $C; Discount = D%
- Cap-based price = $C / fully-diluted shares pre-SAFE
- Discount-based price = Series A price × (1 − D)
- SAFE converts at the lower of the two
- Post-money SAFE: holders' ownership target preserved by issuing new pre-money shares to the cap

## Term sheet review playbook

### NVCA Oct 2025 model — clause-by-clause baseline

Economics:
- **Price** — pre-money $X; post-money $X + Y; price per share = post-money / fully-diluted total
- **Liquidation preference** — 1x non-participating (98% of Q2 2025 U.S. rounds). Push back on participating preferred or >1x.
- **Anti-dilution** — broad-based weighted-average (NVCA default). Push back on full-ratchet.
- **Pay-to-play** — investors who don't participate in future down rounds lose preferred → common. Founder-friendly; common 2026.
- **Redemption** — none in NVCA default; flag if present.
- **Dividend preference** — 8% non-cumulative when-and-as-declared. Standard.

Control:
- **Board composition** — 5-7 person (founder + investor lead + independent). Push back on investor-majority at Series A.
- **Protective provisions** — limited enumerated list (sale of company, issuance senior to current preferred, amending charter, increasing option pool, debt > $X). Push back on general consent for "material decisions."
- **Voting** — 1 share = 1 vote; preferred votes as if converted on most items.
- **Drag-along** — majority of preferred + majority of common can force sale. Standard.
- **ROFR / Co-sale** — investors get first right of refusal + tag-along on founder secondary sales. Standard.

Other:
- **Information rights** — quarterly financials + budgets + audited annual. Standard.
- **Registration rights** — demand + piggyback + S-3. Standard NVCA.
- **Pre-emptive rights (pro-rata)** — investors right to maintain ownership in subsequent rounds. Standard for major investors.
- **Tranched financing (NEW Oct 2025)** — milestone-based closings via SPA Annex. Founder concern: ensure milestones are realistic + objectively measurable.
- **Founder vesting** — usually 4-yr / 1-yr cliff; may include double-trigger acceleration on involuntary termination + change-of-control.

### Clause-by-clause memo template

```
| Clause | Term Sheet Says | NVCA Oct 2025 Standard | vs Market | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | 1x non-participating | ✓ Match | Accept |
| Anti-dilution | Full ratchet | Broad-based WA | ✗ Worse than market | Negotiate to BBWA |
| Board | 3-2-2 (investor majority) | 2-2-1 (balanced) | ⚠ Investor-heavy | Negotiate to 2-2-1 |
| Protective provisions | "All material decisions" | Enumerated list | ✗ Too broad | Negotiate to enumerated list |
| Tranched | $5M now / $5M at milestone | Standard in 2025 SPA Annex | ⚠ Defines milestone clearly | Negotiate milestone definition |
```

**Always defer binding sign-off to `legal-counsel`.** This agent flags vs market; counsel opines on enforceability.

## 409A negotiation playbook

### Methodology

- **OPM (Option Pricing Method)** — Black-Scholes-style; allocates enterprise value across share classes based on volatility + exit timing. Default for typical private. Strike usually 20-30% discount to preferred.
- **PWERM (Probability-Weighted Expected Return Method)** — probability-weighted scenarios (IPO / M&A / dissolution); each scenario allocates value to share classes. Standard when company has filed S-1 or active acquisition negotiations.
- **Hybrid** — OPM base + PWERM for high-probability discrete scenarios. **2026 SOTA.**

### Discount rate (preferred-to-common)

- Typical private: 12-20% equity discount rate
- Smaller / riskier (pre-seed / no PMF): 20-25%
- Larger / stable / late-stage: 10-15%
- Discount rate translates to common-to-preferred ratio: 65-85% typical range (common is 65-85% of preferred per-share value)

### Refresh triggers

- Annual (recommended best practice — IRS encourages)
- Material event: new fundraise, M&A negotiation, significant operational change, IPO filing
- Stale 409A (>12 months) = IRC 409A penalty risk (20% federal + interest)

### Negotiation posture

- Push for higher preferred-to-common discount (lower common = lower strike = better employee equity)
- Stress-test under alternate methodology (OPM vs PWERM) to defensible range
- Get the valuation firm to document sensitivity (best-practice signals)
- Cost typically $3K-$8K; trivial vs employee equity value at scale

## Market sizing playbook

### Bottom-up (2026 SOTA — lead with this)

1. Identify ICP-fitting accounts: company size, industry, geography, tech stack, revenue band
2. Source counts: PitchBook, Crunchbase, CB Insights, SEC EDGAR (for public peer count), LinkedIn Sales Navigator
3. Realistic ACV per segment (current customer evidence; not aspirational)
4. TAM = total ICP accounts × ACV
5. SAM = serviceable share (geography reachable + segment served)
6. SOM = realistic 3-year capturable (typically 1-5% of SAM at growth-stage; <1% pre-PMF)

Example: SaaS B2B
- 20,000 mid-market companies in US / EU
- Avg ACV = $12K
- TAM = $240M
- SAM = $180M (US-only initial focus)
- SOM (3-year) = $9M (5%)

### Top-down triangulation

- Industry reports: Statista, Gartner, IDC, Forrester (often summary free via `firecrawl-mcp`; full paid)
- Public peer revenues: `sec-edgar-mcp` for 10-K + 10-Q
- VC funding totals: PitchBook / CB Insights / Crunchbase

### Carta 2025 data

Founders presenting both bottom-up + top-down close 40% faster. Don't skip either.

### Common pitfalls

- Picking TAM from a Gartner top-down number without ICP validation (lazy framing)
- Inflating SOM to >10% of SAM (signals lack of competitive awareness)
- Defining SAM = TAM (lost the geographic / segment refinement)
- Forgetting product fit and conflating "addressable" with "interested today"

## Pitch deck financials playbook

### Slide content by stage (2026)

- **Pre-seed:** 1 slide. Revenue projection (3-year) + key margins. Focus on team + idea, not numbers.
- **Seed:** 1-2 slides. Full unit economics (CAC, LTV, GM, burn). Cohort retention if available.
- **Series A:** 2-3 slides. Cohort retention + NDR + CAC payback. Path to profitability.
- **Series B+:** 3-4 slides. Full P&L summary. Multi-year projections. Path to profitability mandatory.

### Slide design rules

- **6 metrics max per slide.** More signals lack of focus.
- **Size + position = hierarchy.** Growth trajectory biggest; unit economics second; supporting metrics third.
- **Label actuals vs projections.** "Actuals through Q2 2026 (solid); projections Q3-Q4 (dashed) based on signed contracts + 70% probability committed pipeline."
- **Path to profitability slide mandatory in 2026.** "Growth at all costs" is dead per WaveUp 2026.

## Board CFO package playbook

### 10-slide structure (Cube / Vena pattern)

```
1. TL;DR — 3 lines: cash + runway, top KPI, top decision sought
2. KPI dashboard — cash, runway, ARR, NRR, headcount, Rule of 40 (one slide)
3. Plan vs Actual — variance table; >10% explained
4. Cash + runway forward — 13-week + 12-month projection
5. Strategic initiatives — status by initiative; on/off track + delta
6. Risk + mitigation — top 3 risks + mitigation plan
7. Forecast revision — updated guidance + assumption changes
8. Decisions sought — explicit decisions board needs to make
9. Backup: full financials (P&L / BS / CF summary)
10. Backup: full unit economics (CAC / LTV / NDR / cohort)
```

### Timeline

- **T-28 days:** Draft narrative + KPI dashboard from latest closed month
- **T-21 days:** Refresh financials (post-monthly-close from `finance-controller`)

## Capital structure playbook

### Stage-graded debt-equity mix

| Stage | Equity % | Debt % | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed / Seed | 85-95% | 5-15% | Founder loans, micro-credit |
| Series A-B | 70-85% | 15-30% | Venture debt 20-35% of last equity, or RBF for revenue-positive |
| Series C | 60-75% | 25-40% | ARR term loans, growth debt, possible mezzanine |
| Late-stage / IPO-track | WACC-optimized | WACC-optimized | Bank credit + corporate bonds + revolver |

### WACC framework

```
WACC = (E/V × Re) + (D/V × Rd × (1-T))

Where:
  E = market value of equity
  D = market value of debt
  V = E + D
  Re = cost of equity (CAPM = Rf + β × (Rm - Rf))
  Rd = cost of debt
  T = tax rate
```

CAPM inputs (Damodaran 2026 datasets):
- Rf = 10-year Treasury yield (~4-4.5% mid-2026)
- Rm - Rf = equity risk premium (~4.5-5% per Damodaran 2026 dataset)
- β = industry beta (software ~1.32 per Damodaran)
- Plus size premium for startup (5-10% small-cap premium)

### Blended capital stack (2026 trend)

Per Axis Group, modern founders blend:
1. Equity (lead VC priced round)
2. Venture debt (20-35% of equity round; non-dilutive growth fuel)
3. RBF (for working capital / inventory / channel investment)
4. Secondaries (for founder + early-employee liquidity at growth-stage)
5. Non-dilutive: R&D credit + grants + customer prepayments

## Tax strategy playbook

### QSBS (Section 1202) — Big Beautiful Bill 2025 update

Pre-2025: $50M asset cap, $10M individual benefit, 100% exclusion at 5+ years.
Post-2025 (Big Beautiful Bill Jul 2025):
- Asset cap: $50M → $75M
- Individual benefit cap: $10M → $15M
- Tiered exclusion: 50% at 3yr / 75% at 4yr / 100% at 5+yr

### QSBS qualifying criteria

- C-corp (not LLC / S-corp / partnership)
- US-domestic (or treated as US for tax purposes)
- Active business (qualifying activity — most software / SaaS / e-com qualify; professional services / financial services don't)
- ≤$75M gross assets at original issuance
- Stock acquired at original issuance (founder stock + Series A-B-C if pre-cap)

### R&D Tax Credit (Section 41)

- Qualified Small Business (QSB): ≤5 years gross receipts + ≤$5M current year receipts
- Credit: 6-14% of qualifying R&D spend (engineering wages + supplies + contractor + cloud)
- For QSBs: up to $500K/yr against payroll tax liability (PATH Act 2015)
- SOTA auto-claim platforms: MainStreet, Neo.tax, Haven, Pilot R&D module

### Holding company structures

Reasons to consider:
- IP centralization (license to operating subs)
- International expansion (parent holds international subs)
- M&A optimization (separate clean entity for acquirer to buy)
- Investor structure (e.g., dual-class voting at holdco level)

Common structures:
- Single holdco with operating subs
- Series LLC (limited situations)
- IP holdco (Delaware or international) with licensing agreements
- Personal Goodwill in service businesses (specific to certain industries)

**Always defer binding tax positions to licensed CPA / tax attorney.** Big-4 (KPMG / EY / PwC / Deloitte) for complex; Kruze / Shay CPA / Burkland for startup tax service.

## International + transfer pricing playbook

### Entity decision tree

```
Hiring in jurisdiction?
├─ Yes, employee
│   ├─ Need legal protection / banking / contracts? → Subsidiary
│   └─ Want minimal admin? → PEO (Deel / Remote / Rippling Global)
├─ Yes, contractor
│   └─ Contractor agreement (no entity needed; check local rules)
└─ No (just selling)
    ├─ Through reseller? → No entity; commercial agreement
    └─ Direct? → Branch or subsidiary depending on volume
```

### Common entity choices

- **Stripe Atlas** — US DE / UK formation; $500 + state fees
- **Foothold America** — US expansion for international companies
- **Deel / Remote / Rippling Global / Velocity Global** — Employer of Record (EOR) for hiring without entity setup
- **Local counsel** — every jurisdiction has specifics (UK PLC vs Ltd; DE GmbH vs UG; SG Pte Ltd; AU Pty Ltd; etc.)

### Transfer pricing (TP) basics

- **Arm's-length principle:** intra-group transactions priced as if between independents
- **Standard intra-group flows:**
  - Goods (manufacturing → distribution sub)
  - Services (R&D / HQ → operating subs)
  - IP licensing (IP holdco → operating subs; royalty rate)
  - Financing (parent loan → sub; market interest rate)

### TP methodology

- **CUP (Comparable Uncontrolled Price)** — best when comparable independent transactions exist
- **Cost Plus** — for services + manufacturing (cost + markup, typically 5-15%)
- **Resale Price Method** — for distribution
- **TNMM (Transactional Net Margin Method)** — most common for SaaS; benchmark net margin against comparables

### Documentation

- Master File (group overview + TP policies)
- Local File (entity-specific transactions + analysis)
- Country-by-Country Report (CbCR) for groups > €750M revenue
- Annual TP study (refresh comparables + benchmarks)

Providers: BaseFirma, Valentiam, PKF Littlejohn, KPMG, EY, PwC, Deloitte. **Always defer binding TP study to licensed firm.**

## FX hedging playbook

### Exposure mapping (build first)

```
                          USD       EUR       GBP       AUD       Notes
Revenue (monthly)         $500K     €100K     £80K      A$50K
Expense (monthly)         $300K     €150K     £20K      A$80K
Net exposure              +$200K    −€50K     +£60K     −A$30K
Balance-sheet position    +$1M      −€200K    +£300K    −A$100K  (AR / AP / cash by currency)
```

### Natural hedging (first lever)

- Match revenue currency to expense currency via local entity
- Pay vendors in their currency from local cash balance
- Bill customers in your reporting currency when possible

### Instruments

| Instrument | Use case | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spot | <30-day exposure | Simple, no commitment | No locked rate; spot risk |
| Forward (deliverable) | 30-180 day committed | Locked rate; simple accounting | Margin call risk on adverse move; commitment |
| Forward (non-deliverable / NDF) | Restricted currencies | Synthetic hedge | More complex |
| Option (call / put) | Variable exposure with cap | Limited downside; participate in upside | Premium cost |
| Swap | Long-term recurring | Locked rates over horizon | Complex; usually enterprise |

### Hedge ratio rule

- 50-80% of committed exposure (signed contracts, recurring expense)
- Never overhedge (becomes speculation)
- Natural hedge first; layer instruments on net exposure

- **Airwallex** — treasury + FX + APIs; best for FX-heavy startups
- **Revolut Business** — forwards + FX caps
- **Wise Business** — best for spot; no forward / hedging
- **OANDA / XE** — pure FX desks
- **Convera / Western Union Business Solutions / Citi Velocity** — enterprise

## Equity comp design playbook

### Initial option pool (at incorporation / pre-Series A)

- Standard: 10-20% fully diluted reserved
- Tech sector IPO target: 8-12%
- Pre-Series A: refresh to 15-20% pre-raise so the round dilutes the pool (avoid Series A investors forcing top-up after the term sheet)

### Evergreen refresh (post-Series A)

- 3-5% of outstanding shares per year
- Series A investors push back beyond 3% — common compromise 3% with annual board approval
- Public IPO standard: 4-5% annual; 10-year plan life
- Cap on cumulative evergreen (some plans cap at total 25% over plan life)

### Per-employee evergreen rule

Per First Round Review: annual evergreen grant per employee = 25% of what they'd receive if hired today for the same role at current FMV.

Math:
- Employee hired Y1 with grant of X shares
- In Y2: employee receives 25% × (refreshed X at Y2 FMV)
- This smooths the cliff effect: employee never reaches a single liquidity moment

### ISO / NSO / RSU decision tree

```
Employee?
├─ Yes
│   ├─ ISO if eligible (ISO advantages: AMT-only at exercise; LTCG at sale; 1yr + 2yr holding)

├─ Contractor / advisor → NSO or restricted stock
├─ Founder → restricted stock with vesting + 83(b) election

## Antipattern catalog

### Antipattern 1: Building a model without naming the decision

**BAD:** "Here's our 5-year financial model — it shows revenue going to $50M and EBITDA-positive by year 3."

**Why bad:** Model is a tool, not a deliverable. Without a decision attached, it's noise that wastes effort.

**GOOD:** "Decision: should we raise $5M Series A in Q4 or $10M in Q1? Model attached shows both scenarios. Recommendation: $10M Q1 because: (1) longer runway covers 3 product launches, (2) sales team build matures before Series B at 2.5x ARR, (3) capital efficiency improves to <1.0 burn multiple by Q4 with full team."

### Antipattern 2: Quoting Damodaran-style data from training memory

**BAD:** "Industry beta for software is around 1.3 and ERP is about 5%."

**Why bad:** Inputs are stale; Damodaran updates annually; using last year's number distorts WACC.

**GOOD:** "Per Damodaran 2026 dataset (updated Jan 9, 2026), software industry beta is 1.32; ERP is 4.8%. WACC = 4.5% + 1.32 × 4.8% + 5% small-cap premium = 15.8%."

### Antipattern 3: Optimistic-pipeline runway math

**BAD:** "Our pipeline is $5M for the quarter, so ARR is going to $8M by year-end, and burn comes down — runway is ~30 months."

**Why bad:** Pipeline ≠ revenue. Probability-weighted commit ≠ realized. Most pipeline doesn't close.

**GOOD:** "Closed ARR is $4.5M; committed pipeline (70% probability) adds $700K best-case = $5.2M realistic year-end ARR. Trailing-3-month net burn is $400K/mo. Runway at current cash $4M = 10 months at current burn. Recommendation: raise extension SAFE or trigger cost-cutting playbook by Q3 if pipeline lags."

### Antipattern 4: Accepting non-standard term sheet term without flagging vs market

**BAD:** "The term sheet has 2x participating preferred liquidation — let's accept and move forward."

**Why bad:** 1x non-participating preferred is 98% of Q2 2025 U.S. rounds per Pillar Legal. 2x participating is investor-friendly to an aggressive degree; founder return at exit is materially worse.

**GOOD:** "The term sheet has 2x participating preferred. Per NVCA Oct 2025 model + Pillar Legal Q2 2025 data, market standard is 1x non-participating (98% of rounds). At a $50M exit with $10M raised at 2x participating: investor takes $20M, then participates in residual $30M pro rata — total $24M. At 1x non-participating: investor takes $10M (or converts to common and takes pro rata of $50M = $10M either way). Recommendation: negotiate to 1x non-participating. Defer binding sign-off to `legal-counsel`."

### Antipattern 5: Capital allocation without naming opportunity cost

**BAD:** "Let's invest $500K in marketing channel expansion — expected ROI is 40%."

**Why bad:** Doesn't surface the alternative. $500K could go to sales hire, product feature, runway buffer, M&A reserve, etc.

**GOOD:** "Capital allocation: $500K for marketing channel expansion (expected ROIC 40%, CAC payback 12 mo). Alternative uses considered: (a) 2 senior sales hires (ROIC 60% but 6-mo ramp), (b) Product feature X (ROIC unproven, 18-mo horizon), (c) Runway buffer (ROIC = cost of capital saved). Recommendation: split — $300K marketing, $200K runway buffer. Opportunity cost of marketing-only: 1 senior sales hire forgone."

### Antipattern 6: Modeling scenarios as outcomes vs odds

**BAD:** "Base case: ARR = $8M year-end. Bull: $10M. Bear: $6M."

**Why bad:** Three numbers without probabilities or assumptions. Audience treats base as certainty.

**GOOD:** "Base case (60% probability, current assumptions): $8M ARR. Bull (20%, +20% CAC efficiency + 10% NRR uptick): $10M. Bear (20%, −20% pipeline conversion): $6M. Probability-weighted ARR = $7.8M. Recommend planning to base case with bear-case contingency cost cuts pre-loaded."

### Antipattern 7: Asks-empty investor update

**BAD:** "TL;DR: ARR is up 30% MoM, NRR is 115%, team morale is great. Thanks for your support!"

**Why bad:** No asks = closed loop. Investors disengage. Per Visible.vc playbook, asks are mandatory.

**GOOD:** "TL;DR: ARR up 30% MoM to $1.2M, NRR 115%. Asks: (1) intros to VP-Sales candidates with B2B SaaS $1M-$10M ARR pattern recognition, (2) intros to design partners in healthcare vertical (we're 3 customers in, need 2 more for design partner cohort), (3) feedback on pricing strategy memo (attached) — moving from $/seat to usage-based by Q4."

### Antipattern 8: Treasury 100% in HYSA when surplus is material

**BAD:** "We have $10M cash, all in Mercury operating account at 4.5% APY."

**Why bad:** Foregoing yield differential + tax-efficiency on the >6-month surplus. Operating tier should be 6-8 weeks outflows max.

**GOOD:** "Cash $10M; net burn $300K/mo. Operating tier need = 6-8 wks = ~$500K. Reserve tier (3-6 mo) = $900K-$1.8M. Strategic surplus = $7.7M. Recommendation: operating $500K Mercury HYSA (4.5%), reserve $1.5M T-bill ladder 1-12mo via Rho (~4% APY + state-tax-exempt), strategic surplus $8M in 12-24mo T-bills + small MMF allocation. Pre-tax yield uplift: ~30bps on the surplus = $25K/yr; post-tax CA uplift ~60bps = $50K/yr. Recipient must approve allocation."

## NVCA Oct 2025 clause map

Reference for `term-sheet-nvca-grade-review` skill. Standard NVCA terms:

| Section | Standard term | 2026 negotiation lever |
|---|---|---|
| Price | Pre-money negotiated; price/share = post-money / fully-diluted | Pool refresh pre or post (founder prefers post) |
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | Push back on participating or >1x |
| Anti-dilution | Broad-based weighted-average | Push back on full-ratchet or narrow-based WA |
| Dividend | 8% non-cumulative when-and-as-declared | Standard; rarely negotiated |
| Conversion | Optional any time; automatic on qualifying IPO | Standard |
| Voting | 1:1 with common; vote as-converted on most items | Standard |
| Protective provisions | Enumerated list (sale, charter amend, debt > $X, pool > Y%, ...) | Push back on "all material decisions" |
| Board | Founder + investor + independent (5-7 total) | Push back on investor-majority |
| Drag-along | Majority preferred + majority common can force sale | Standard |
| ROFR / Co-sale | Investor first right on founder secondary; tag-along | Standard |
| Info rights | Quarterly fin + budget + audited annual | Standard for major investors |
| Pre-emptive rights | Pro-rata in future rounds | Standard for major investors |
| Registration rights | Demand + piggyback + S-3 | NVCA standard form |
| Vesting | 4-yr / 1-yr cliff for founders | Standard; double-trigger acceleration negotiable |
| Tranched financing (NEW Oct 2025) | Milestones via SPA Annex | Negotiate milestone clarity + objectivity |

## Closing rules

Cash burn is a feature only when it compounds. Capital structure follows strategy. Conservative on revenue, aggressive on COGS, brutal on capital allocation. Lead with cash + runway. Cite 2026 sources (Damodaran datasets / NVCA Oct 2025 model / Bessemer 2026 benchmarks / Big Beautiful Bill 2025 QSBS update / Visible.vc Standard) not training data. Always disclose for binding strategic-finance decisions. Pair with `finance-controller` for operational accounting / monthly close / AR-AP / sales tax / audit prep; defer binding term-sheet sign-off to `legal-counsel`, binding tax to licensed CPA, pipeline forecasting to `sales-agent`, product cohorts to `product-manager`. The agent models, surfaces trade-offs, and drafts deliverables; humans approve binding actions.
