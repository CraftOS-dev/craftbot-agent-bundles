## Monthly investor update playbook

### Visible.vc Standard template (verbatim section structure)

```
SUBJECT: [Company] Monthly Update — [Month YYYY]

# TL;DR (mandatory; lead with cash + runway)
- Cash: $X.XM (as of [date])
- Runway: XX months @ current burn ($X.XM/mo)
- [1 sentence on the period's headline metric]

# Key Metrics (3-5 KPIs max)
| Metric | This month | Last month | Δ | vs Plan |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ARR | $X | $Y | +Z% | +/- W% |
| Customers | N | M | +K | +/- L |
| MRR Churn | X% | Y% | ... | ... |
| Cash burn | $X | $Y | ... | ... |
| Headcount | N | M | ... | ... |

# Highlights (3-5; lead with the most material)
- ...

# Lowlights (mandatory non-empty 3-5; bad news first)
- ...

# Asks (mandatory non-empty)
- Intros to [specific target accounts / firms]
- Talent referrals for [specific roles]
- Advice on [specific decision]

# Financials snapshot
[Optional 1-paragraph commentary or a small P&L excerpt]

# Looking forward
[1-2 sentences on next 30 days]

Thanks for the support,
[Founder/CEO name]
```

### Cadence calendar (2026 SOTA private-co)

- **Monthly** — pre-seed to Series A actively raising or recently raised
- **Quarterly** — Series B+ steady-state OR pre-IPO with formal quarterly cadence
- **Ad-hoc** — material events (round close, big customer win, key hire, churn, reorg, fundraise opening)

### Anti-patterns

- Empty lowlights section ("no concerns this month") — investors smell it; you lose credibility.
- Empty asks section — closes the loop; investors stop helping.
- Burying cash + runway below the highlights — TL;DR must lead.
- Tone shift period-over-period without naming why.

## Quarterly board + investor letter playbook

### Structure (institutional cadence)

1. **CEO opening narrative** (2-3 paragraphs) — period summary + strategic frame
2. **Strategy update against OKRs / pillars** — pillar-by-pillar with green/yellow/red status
3. **Financial highlights** — revenue, ARR, gross margin, burn multiple, runway (private) OR YoY/sequential revenue, EPS, segment, guidance (public)
4. **KPI scorecard vs plan** — table format
5. **People** — hires of note + departures (executives, board)
6. **Risks + mitigations** — top 3-5, each with named mitigation
7. **Asks** — non-empty

### Timeline

- **T-21**: confirm board meeting date + agenda
- **T-14**: outline + financial highlights from `finance-agent`
- **T-10**: draft circulated to CEO + CFO
- **T-7**: pre-wire individual board members on contentious items
- **T-3**: freeze
- **T-0 (board)**: deliver
- **T+3**: sanitize + send to broader investor base

## Earnings call script playbook

### Standard structure (public-co; 60-90 min total)

```
00:00 - 02:00  Operator + IR open ("Safe Harbor statement + Reg G reconciliation
               available in the press release on our IR website")
02:00 - 07:00  CEO open (strategic + thematic; year-in-context + Q-in-context)
07:00 - 15:00  CFO walkthrough (revenue + margin + segment + capital allocation
               + guidance)
15:00 - 60:00  Q&A — operator routes to sell-side analysts in queue order
               (typically 6-12 analysts get questions; IR keeps roster)
60:00 - 65:00  Closing remarks (CEO 1-2 minutes); operator close
```

### Q&A binder (50-150 anticipated Qs)

Categorize by topic:
- Guidance (range vs point; assumptions; sensitivities)
- Revenue / segment (drivers; cohorts; mix; pricing)
- Margin / unit econ (gross margin path; OpEx leverage; rule of 40)
- Capital allocation (buyback; dividend; M&A; reinvestment)
- M&A (pipeline; integration; synergies)
- Competitive (key competitor moves; win/loss; market share)
- ESG / governance (climate; DEI; board; exec comp; ISS/Glass Lewis)
- Macro / regulatory (rates; tariffs; FX; reg risk)
- People (key hires; executive transitions; succession)
- Long-term thesis bridges (every A bridges back to the long-term)

Each Q has:
- Vetted A (50-200 words)
- Bridge to long-term thesis (1 sentence)
- Owner (CEO / CFO / IR for routing)
- As-of date (last refreshed)
- Source citation (model / external)

## Guidance setting playbook

### Decision tree (2026)

```
Has consensus formed?
├── No (S-1 process; first 2-3 quarters post-IPO)
│   ├── Issue range guidance with wide bands; signal modeling tightening over time
│
├── Yes (steady-state)
│   ├── Confidence intervals tight (±3% revenue, ±5% EPS)?
│   │   ├── Issue point guidance — signals high conviction
│   │
│   ├── Confidence intervals wide (±10% or more)?
│   │   ├── Issue range guidance — protects against miss
│   │
│   ├── Highly volatile sector / segment (commodity-linked, FX-exposed)?
│   │   ├── Issue range guidance with explicit FX / commodity assumptions
```

### Beat-and-raise cadence (2026 institutional preference)

- Set Q-1 guidance conservatively (60-70% confidence at low end of range)
- Beat 2-5% on revenue; raise FY guidance by half the beat
- Beat 5%+ on revenue; consider raising whole-year by the full beat
- Miss: lower guidance immediately, narrate carefully, never claim "one-time" without rigor

### Pre-guidance straw poll

- Top 3-5 analysts (highest influence): brief 1:1 ~10 days before
- "If we land at $X revenue / $Y EPS / [KPI Z], what's the model reaction?" (Miss / land / beat — they speak directionally without committing)
- Counsel sign-off on the line of questioning — not a Reg FD shop-around, just calibration

## 13F monitoring playbook

### Monthly cadence

```python
# Pull latest 13Fs via sec-edgar-mcp
import sec_edgar_mcp
filings = sec_edgar_mcp.fetch_forms(
    form_type="13F-HR",
    period=last_quarter_end,
    cik=ALL_INSTITUTIONS_OVER_100M
)

# Filter for filings holding our ticker
our_filings = [f for f in filings if "OUR_CIK" in f.holdings]

# Compute deltas vs prior quarter
deltas = compute_deltas(this_q=our_filings, prior_q=prior_filings)

# Flag: new initiations, ≥25% size changes, full exits
flags = (
    [d for d in deltas if d.type == "NEW"] +
    [d for d in deltas if abs(d.pct_change) >= 0.25] +
    [d for d in deltas if d.type == "EXIT"]
)
```

### Outreach decisions

- New initiation: send IR welcome packet within 30 days (deck + 4-quarter financials + recent press releases + Q&A FAQ)
- Large add: schedule 1:1 within 60 days; brief on multi-quarter thesis
- Exit: respectful 1:1 within 90 days (understand why; capture for thesis-evolution insight; not "win them back" hard sell)
- 13D/G filer (>5%): escalate to CEO/CFO/counsel immediately — activist diligence

## Quiet period playbook

### Decision tree

```
Is this a public-company question with material financial info?
├── No → respond normally
├── Yes → are we currently in quiet period (T-~14 days to earnings call)?
│   ├── No → respond per Reg FD playbook (simultaneous public release if material)
│   ├── Yes → are we asked to refine prior guidance or share new financial info?
│   │   ├── Yes → REFUSE + route to counsel + auto-reply with quiet-period date
│   │   ├── No (housekeeping / scheduling) → respond minimally + flag to counsel
```

### Outbound auto-reply template

```
Thank you for reaching out. [Company] is currently in our quiet period
ahead of our [Q-X YYYY] earnings call scheduled for [date].

During this period, we are not able to discuss new financial information
or refinements to our previously issued guidance.

We look forward to picking up our conversation after the call. If you have
non-financial scheduling questions, please contact [IR ops alias].

— [IR contact]
```

### Reg FD audit trail

- Every quiet-period outbound logged in `notion-mcp` Reg FD register
- Counsel monthly review of register for pattern issues

## Embargoed disclosure playbook

### When permitted (counsel-driven; rare)

- M&A counterparty diligence pre-announcement (under NDA + counsel signoff)
- IPO syndicate pre-launch (managing underwriters + their selected accounts)
- Rare: pre-earnings preview to a tightly limited group (almost never; counsel cross-check mandatory)

### NEVER permitted

- Selective preview to a favored analyst to help model
- Off-the-record number sharing with a fund just because they're a top holder
- Pre-disclosure to journalists for "exclusive" framing of bad news

### Mechanics

1. Identify trigger + counsel signoff
2. NDA signed by recipient BEFORE info shared
3. Embargo timing locked (e.g., "embargo lifts at 4:01 PM ET on [date]")
4. Recipient list documented in `notion-mcp` Reg FD register
5. Wire release at embargo lift to make info Reg-FD-compliant

## Antipattern catalog

### Antipattern 1: Refining guidance in the quiet period

**BAD:**
> [In a 1:1 with sell-side analyst during quiet period]
> "Off the record, we're tracking ahead of the bottom end of our prior guide."

**Why it's bad:** Reg FD violation (selective disclosure of material non-public info). Quiet period violation. Career-ending.

**GOOD:**
> "Thanks for reaching out — we're in our quiet period until [date]. I'll follow up post-call. In the meantime, our [date]-stated guide is the most current public view."

### Antipattern 2: Empty lowlights section

**BAD:**
> **Lowlights**
> No material concerns this month.

**Why it's bad:** Investors don't believe it; they assume hiding. Credibility ratchets down.

**GOOD:**
> **Lowlights**
> 1. Enterprise close rate dropped from 32% to 24% in mid-funnel — driver appears to be procurement-cycle elongation per recent renewals.
> 2. Senior engineering hire pulled out of accepted offer (signing-bonus-driven counter); slot remains open.
> 3. Two top customers reduced seat count from 50→40 each — pricing pressure during renewal.

### Antipattern 3: Burying cash + runway

**BAD:**
> [Update lead-in talks about a customer win + a hire + a strategic partnership before mentioning cash position 12 paragraphs in]

**Why it's bad:** Investors scan for cash first. Burying signals manipulation.

**GOOD:**
> # TL;DR
> - Cash: $4.2M (as of [date])
> - Runway: 14 months @ $300K/mo burn
> [...other content follows]

### Antipattern 4: Quoting consensus from memory

**BAD:**
> "Sell-side consensus is around $230M for Q2."

**Why it's bad:** No source, no as-of date, no precision. Could be stale; could be wrong; could be cherry-picked.

**GOOD:**
> "Per FactSet consensus pulled 2026-06-09 (n=8 analysts), Q2 revenue $233.4M (range $228-238M); EPS $0.45 (range $0.42-0.48)."

### Antipattern 5: Selective enrichment to favored analyst

**BAD:**
> [In 1:1] "Off the record, our enterprise pipeline closed 25% faster this quarter — should help your model."

**Why it's bad:** Reg FD violation. Career-ending.

**GOOD:**
> "Public disclosure on pipeline velocity will be in our Q-X earnings press release / call. Happy to walk through our previously disclosed enterprise framework if useful."

### Antipattern 6: Skipping Reg G reconciliation

**BAD:**
> [Press release reports "Adjusted EBITDA $25M" without a GAAP reconciliation table]

**Why it's bad:** Reg G requires non-GAAP measures be reconciled to nearest GAAP measure with equal prominence. SEC enforcement risk.

**GOOD:**
> [Press release includes a "Reconciliation of GAAP to Non-GAAP Measures" table at the bottom, with operating income / loss bridging to Adjusted EBITDA, line items labeled and explained.]

### Antipattern 7: Issuing 8-K without counsel review

**BAD:**
> [IR drafts an 8-K Item 7.01 Reg FD disclosure announcing a strategic partnership and submits via EDGAR without counsel review.]

**Why it's bad:** Securities law liability. Reg FD specific risks. Counsel must approve every binding filing.

**GOOD:**
> [IR drafts the 8-K + accompanying press release; routes to `legal-counsel` for review; counsel makes redlines; IR finalizes; counsel files on EDGAR.]

### Antipattern 8: Tone shift period-over-period without naming why

**BAD:**
> [Last update: "Tracking well to plan." This update: "We continue to invest in long-term durable growth." Investors notice; nobody explains.]

**Why it's bad:** Investors read tone shifts as signals. Silence breeds speculation.

**GOOD:**
> [In TL;DR: "Note tone shift from prior update — Q-on-Q revenue +12% vs +18% prior quarter reflects pull-forward dynamics rather than fundamental softening. Detail in metrics section below."]

## NIRI Standards reference (excerpt)

> Full reference at https://www.niri.org/standards-of-practice. IR career bar.

Key NIRI Standards relevant to this agent:

1. **Disclosure** — material info promptly + accurately + fairly + simultaneously.
2. **Reg FD compliance** — never selective; document Reg FD cross-checks.
3. **Quiet period** — institutional practice; not legally mandated but Reg-FD-adjacent.
4. **Earnings disclosure** — press release + 8-K + conference call + IR website + EDGAR linkout simultaneous.
5. **Analyst relations** — even-handed; coverage agnostic to stance; never selective enrichment.
6. **Investor communications** — clear + balanced + forward-looking with Safe Harbor.
7. **ESG / sustainability** — disclosed per framework choice + consistent with operations.

## Item 402(v) Pay-versus-Performance walkthrough

> SEC's 2025+ proxy disclosure requirement.

### Required table

```
| Year | Summary Comp (PEO) | Compensation Actually Paid (PEO) | Avg Summary Comp (NEOs) | Avg CAP (NEOs) | TSR | Peer TSR | Net Income | Company KPI |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2025 | $X | $Y | $A | $B | +Z% | +W% | $N | [KPI] |
| ...  | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
```

### Mechanics

- 5 years of data (transitional rule: first year discloses 3)
- "Compensation Actually Paid" (CAP) = Summary Comp adjusted for:
  - Pension value changes
  - Equity award fair-value changes during reporting year
- TSR per company-selected peer group OR industry index
- Company-selected financial KPI (most important non-GAAP measure in pay decisions)

### Common pitfalls

- Using wrong peer group (must match the one in CD&A pay-mix discussion)
- Forgetting fair-value adjustments for outstanding equity awards
- Inconsistent treatment of CEO transitions during the year

## ISS / Glass Lewis 2026 policy delta

### ISS 2026 policy updates (recurring annual refresh — track each Nov for next-Jan effective)

- Climate disclosure expectations expanded (per IFRS S2)
- Board gender diversity (continued — typically need ≥30% women)
- Excessive perks scrutiny (CEO non-business air travel; club memberships; tax gross-ups)
- Universal Proxy Card (UPC) — IRS director-by-director vote
- Pay-versus-performance Item 402(v) integration into CD&A scoring

### Glass Lewis 2026 policy updates

- Climate-related risk oversight (board-level explicit)
- Cybersecurity oversight disclosure (post-Item 1C 10-K)
- Auditor tenure scrutiny (>10 years prompts adverse rec)
- DEI expanded — ethnic / racial diversity at board level
- Anti-takeover provisions — adverse on poison pills + classified boards

### Pre-engagement playbook

- File DEF 14A early enough for ISS / Glass Lewis to issue Proxy Paper / Voting Recommendation ≥3 weeks before AGM
- If ISS / Glass Lewis flag a concern, engage early (counsel + IR + comp committee chair)
- Pre-publication 1:1s with ISS / Glass Lewis analysts on contested items
